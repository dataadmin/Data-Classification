Categorizing and Securing Institutional Data

This is a guide to the process of categorizing university data by the level of risk to ensure that its confidentiality, integrity and appropriate availability are not compromised in the running of university business. This process considers that data is protected and in compliance with both government regulations and institutional policies from the time it is created until it is disposed. The four center tabs with lock icons below provide definitions and examples of data at each risk level as indicated by the number of locks. The last tab on the right presents guidelines on how to safeguard data through its lifecycle.

Guidelines for Safeguarding Institutional Data*

      * These Guidelines are for the usage of the Pilot audience only at this time. They do not expressly deal with the development of procedures for vetting
who may have access to certain data types, and they only address high-risk data, i.e. 3 and 4 Lock Data.

Capture -> Paper -> Fax

- Only receive data this way if it is required to meet an essential business need. - Reduce or eliminate receiving data this way where not required to meet an essential business need.
  • - Write "Confidential" on fax cover sheet, when faxing.

  • - Coordinate with the sender so you are available to directly receive the fax when it is sent.

  • - Personnel receiving data this way must have authorization by his/her manager, and/or relevant offices, as necessary (e.g. Human Subject Research Protection, Office of Information Security).

  • - Any applicable consent requirements must be met before data is collected.

  • - Data collection must happen in an environment conducive to maintaining confidentiality. Make sure that the material received is never out of your possesion.

  • - Document must be logged to identify the collector and location of the record. It is recommended to identify the name of individual responsible for receiving as well as date of reception. Location of data and data type must be inventoried and timestamped upon creation; e.g. "[Doc.Name] created on [DD/MM/YYYY], stored in [File Location], contains student grades, [Collector Name]".

INCIDENT REPORT
  • - Security Incidents will be reported as required by State and Federal Law and University Policy.

  • - Immediately report any suspected unauthorized access, compromise, or loss of data to the Office of Information Security

  • - The Office of Information Security has the responsibility to evaluate incidents and to determine if an incident response is necessary.


AUDIT
  • - Each unit or department must conduct an annual review of where data is located, who has access to it, the access control mechanisms, encryption protocols, and data destruction protocols. Need a policy and a link to it

  • - Access to this data needs to be recertified on a timely basis as required by the Internal Audit Department.

  • - Managers are responsible for the ongoing access review of their subordinates to ensure that their access to university data is assigned appropriately.

  • - Each manager is responsible to ensure that his/her staff uses approved university devices to use, capture, store and share this data according to Data Security Lifecycle Phases guidelines.

  • - Data Custodians are responsible for the ongoing access review of their domain data to ensure that access is assigned appropriatelly.

  • - Auditing capability must be in place to adhere to the proper usage, capturing, storing and sharing of this data via Data Security Lifecycle Phases.

  • - Each unit or department must have and review annual procedures for immediately removing access upon change in employment status.
